Health

Balanced Diet

Feed your dog high-quality commercial food appropriate for their age, size, and activity level. Avoid human foods that can be toxic.

Fresh Water

Always provide access to clean, fresh water. Change it at least twice daily and clean the bowl regularly to prevent bacteria.

Weight Management

Monitor your dog's weight and adjust food portions accordingly. Obesity can lead to serious health problems.

Feeding Schedule

Establish a regular feeding routine. Most adult dogs do well with two meals per day, while puppies need more frequent feeding.

Bathing

Bathe your dog every 4-6 weeks or when dirty. Use dog-specific shampoo to maintain healthy skin and coat.

Brushing

Brush your dog regularly to remove loose hair, prevent mats, and distribute natural oils. Frequency depends on coat type.

Dental Care

Brush your dog's teeth several times a week with canine toothpaste. Provide dental chews to help reduce plaque.

Nail Trimming

Trim nails every 3-4 weeks to prevent discomfort and potential injury. Be careful not to cut the quick.

Basic Commands

Teach essential commands like sit, stay, come, and leave it. Use positive reinforcement with treats and praise.

House Training

Establish a routine for bathroom breaks. Take your dog out first thing in the morning, after meals, and before bedtime.

Socialization

Expose your dog to various people, animals, and environments early to develop confidence and good behavior.

Crate Training

Make the crate a positive space with treats and toys. Never use it for punishment. It should feel like a safe den.

Vaccinations

Keep vaccinations up to date as recommended by your vet. Core vaccines protect against serious diseases.

Parasite Control

Use flea, tick, and heartworm prevention year-round. Regularly check for signs of parasites.

Regular Checkups

Schedule annual wellness exams. Senior dogs may need checkups twice yearly to catch age-related issues early.

Exercise

Provide daily physical activity appropriate for your dog's breed, age, and health. Mental stimulation is equally important.
